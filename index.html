Our Vision



To create a welcoming and informative environment for prospective students and community members. Student Ambassadors demonstrate professionalism, dedication, and knowledge of the School's programs and services that contribute to the mission and vision of the School of Computing and Information.

Patrick Healy
(BSIS, BSCS, BSTAT)


I'm a senior, planning to graduate in spring 2019 with three bachelor's degrees in Computer Science, Information Science, and Philosophy. Primarily, I am interested in studying video games, from their effectiveness as educational technology to their impact as pieces of art.

I am a BPhil candidate through the University Honor's College with a research project in gamification. Working with a neuroscience lab on campus, I developed a web-application to teach low-income parents about brain development through short games; my BPhil project is centered around measuring the effectiveness of gamification strategies I designed for the application. I also work as an application developer in a research lab in the History and Philosophy of Science department interested in the historical representation of women in philosophy.

In addition to that research, I am also working with a few student teams on various major video game development projects, including a 'Pokemon: Go'-style location-based augmented reality game for parents to play with their kids, an interactive fiction project I'm developing with English graduate students, and an experimental feminist philosophy game that analyzes arguments around the #metoo movement.

My interests and work in games pushed me to found 'games-edu', a student organization centered on connecting students and faculty to develop educational and transformational games across various disciplines. My goal, both in this organization and in my prospective career, is to research and develop games that improve people's lives, teach them things, and encourage conversations on social justice.

Outside of my academic work, I also find time to sing bass in the Pitt Men's Glee Club and Pantherhythms. In my free time, I take advantage of Pittsburgh's excellent public transportation (free with a Pitt ID!) to explore the cultural district, check out the newest groundbreaking museum exhibits, and continue my search for the city's best pulled-pork sandwich.

I’m extremely thankful for all of the opportunities Pitt has given me over the past few years and I love talking about them with anyone who asks! Feel free to reach out!
